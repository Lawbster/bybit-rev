## Deflationary Mechanisms

- **97% of protocol fees** fund HYPE buybacks on open market, permanently burned
- **HIP-1 auction fees** (token listings) paid in HYPE and burned
- **Staking** to HyperBFT validators reduces effective circulating supply

### Burn Rate (as of Mar/Apr 2026)

- Daily: ~34,500 HYPE burned, ~26,800 distributed = **net removal ~7,700/day**
- Annualized: **~6.15M HYPE/year** net deflationary
- Monthly: ~512k net removed from circulation
- Cumulative buybacks: **>$1.3B** through Assistance Fund
- Buyback intensity: ~7% of market cap annually (4-5x more aggressive than ETH)

### Net Supply Math

- Monthly unlock: ~9.9M HYPE (team vesting)
- Monthly burn: ~0.5M HYPE (net)
- **Net monthly inflation: ~9.4M HYPE** during vesting period
- After 2028 (vesting complete): becomes purely deflationary

## Hyperliquid Assistance Fund (HAF)

- Created after JELLY exploit (March 2025)
- Protocol-controlled fund holding HYPE + USDC
- Backstops insurance fund, covers socialized losses
- Can intervene in markets (used during JELLY incident to close positions)
- Wallet publicly trackable on-chain

## Trading Implications

1. **Monthly sell pressure on 6th**: ~$300M+ worth of team tokens unlock each month. Watch for pre-unlock selling (day 3-5) and post-unlock recovery.
2. **Buyback floor**: 97% fee-funded buybacks provide structural demand. Higher volume = more burns = more support.
3. **After 2028**: Once team vesting ends, HYPE becomes net deflationary purely from fee burns. Long-term structurally bullish on supply.
4. **HAF wildcard**: Protocol can intervene in markets. Tail risk events get backstopped but also means positions can be force-closed.
